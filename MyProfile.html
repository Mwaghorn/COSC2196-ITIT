Project Idea

Overview

In summary the project is designed to help people keep track of all their medical data. The app will hold information inputted by your doctor such as appointment history, upcoming appointments, allergies, dietary requirements, prescriptions and medications. Implemented QR codes for pharmacists to use to obtain your prescription data and push notifications and check boxes to remind and incentivise the users to keep track of and take their medications. Push notifications for upcoming appointments and important information your doctor may need to send you. All accessible on one application making it easier for your doctor and yourself.

Motivation

Almost everyone, even the elderly are using smart phones, either android or IOS, and 65 per cent of adults in the United States are on some form of prescription drugs. Further 91 per cent of people over the age of 80 are on prescription medication (Georgetown University, 2019). Making it easier for these people to keep track of their medication and prescription drugs would decrease the chances of them forgetting what to take and when to take them and by giving them quick and easy access to the information about the medication, they can easily educate themselves on the importance of the drugs. The applications motivation and goal to assist people in living more healthy lives and giving the individual a better understanding of what they are taking.

Description

The app will have the user create an account and input data such as your Medicare and other information such as Birthdate, name and location. Once the user has made an account, a doctor which supports the application can connect with the user and is able to enter information for the user to view, such as prescriptions, a summary of their doctor’s visit, dietary requirements, and the times and amount of the prescription drugs to take.

There will be two separate versions of the app. The doctor’s app and the patient app. For a doctor to create an account they must supply the supporting documentation to prove that they are a doctor, and they are also who they say they are. Once they have an account, they then have a list of their patients which they have connected with and the option to request to add a patient and request to remove a patient. When the doctor needs to send a message or update information for a patient they can simply click on the patient, select the category (prescriptions, dietary, general, etc..) and write the message. Another feature will be a send-all which will let the doctor send out a message to all patients about important information about COVID-19 or other issues. Doctors can also see what other doctors have prescribed and treated you for without having to ask.

From the patient’s perspective they will sign up for the account and after this any outstanding requests for a doctor to connect with them will be shown and must be accepted or declined before continuing. Now the patient will be on the main screen where they will view the applications UI. There will be a list of all the important categories such as, doctors, prescriptions, medication reminders, appointments, personal information and emergency contact numbers like 000, and your local emergency facility. The doctors tab will hold information on all your doctors which you have connected with, including the office they work at and how to get in contact with them. The prescriptions tab will let the patient keep track of their prescriptions, have an image of what the pill will look like and supply links to important resources about the medication. The medication reminders let you view when and how many of your medications you should be taking each day. This is inputted by your doctor to save you the confusion of having to enter it yourself. This includes being set push notifications for when you should be taking the medication. The appointment tab will display all your upcoming and previous appointments as well as who to contact if you have an enquiry (reception or the doctor themselves). The personal information tab will be inputted by yourself and is only visible by the doctors you accept. This includes name, address, age, weight and more.

Tools and Technologies

The application will be available on android and IOS devices for patients but for the doctors they can access it on Windows and OSX as well as they will most likely want to access it from their office. The app needs to be available across all platforms so needs to be developed with a language that enables the app to be Hybrid rather than native. The app will most likely use a language like C# which is an OOP (object-oriented programming) language or a form of JavaScript such as react. The IEE used will most likely be Visual Studio as it has a lot of support and is free.

Outcome

With this application fully developed I believe it can help doctors and patients. Patients having quick and easy access to all the helpful resources they need plus push notifications to make sure they remember to take their medications and doctors now have an easier way to keep track of how the patient is doing in terms of medication, personal information, and previous appointments they have made.
